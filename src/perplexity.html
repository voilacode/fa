PTE Core, developed by Pearson, is a comprehensive English proficiency test recognized by the Canadian government for work, migration, and study purposes.

This fully digital assessment evaluates general English skills across four key areas:

reading, writing, listening, and speaking, offering test-takers results within 48 hours.

PTE Core Exam Format Breakdown

The PTE Core exam is structured into four main sections: Speaking & Writing, Reading, Listening, and an integrated Skills section. Each part assesses different language abilities and is designed to evaluate a candidate's proficiency in practical, non-academic English contexts 1 2

Speaking & Writing:

This section is approximately 50 minutes long and contains seven different question types 3. It assesses both speaking and writing skills through various tasks:

  • Speaking tasks include:
    • Read Aloud
    • Repeat Sentence
    • Describe Image
    • Respond to the Situation (2-4 questions, 20 seconds preparation time, 40 seconds speaking time)
    • Answer Short Question
  • Writing tasks include:
    • Summarize Written Text (1-2 questions, 10 minutes per summary, 25-50 words)
    • Write an Email (2-3 questions, 9 minutes per email, 50-120 words)

    The scoring criteria for speaking focus on effective communication, fluency, correct pronunciation, and intonation. Writing is evaluated based on sentence formation, grammar, spelling, and vocabulary usage 2

    Reading:

    This section consists of five question types that assess reading comprehension and vocabulary knowledge:

    • Fill in the Blanks (Reading & Writing)
    • Multiple Choice – Choose Multiple Answers
    • Describe Image
    • Re-order Paragraph
    • Multiple Choice – Choose a Single Answer

    Listening:

    The Listening section measures the candidate's ability to understand various contexts spoken in different accents. It includes the following task types:

    • Summarize Spoken Text (1-2 questions, 8 minutes per summary, 20-30 words)
    • Multiple Choice – Choose Multiple Answers
    • Fill in the Blanks (Listening)
    • Multiple Choice – Choose a Single Answer
    • Select Missing Word
    • Highlight Incorrect Words (1-2 questions)
    • Write from Dictation

    Integrated Skills:

    While not explicitly mentioned as a separate section, the PTE Core integrates skills across tasks. For example, the Fill in the Blanks (Reading & Writing) task assesses both reading and writing abilities 2

    The PTE Core test is scored out of 90 across all four sections. Each section is scored individually, ranging from 10 to 90. The overall scores are computed based on 10 criteria, including the four section scores and six enabling skills: fluency, pronunciation, written discourse, spelling, vocabulary, and grammar usage 2

    For Canadian visa applications, PTE Core scores are converted to Canadian Language Benchmarks (CLB) scores. The test uses state-of-the-art AI and automated scoring technology, ensuring an unbiased evaluation without human interaction. 2

    Compared to PTE Academic, the PTE Core focuses on non-academic contexts such as broadcasts, announcements, emails, work meetings, telephone assistance, and internet conversations, making it more suitable for general English proficiency assessment 2

    PTE vs PTE Core

    PTE Core and PTE Academic, while both developed by Pearson, have several key differences that test-takers should be aware of:

    • Purpose and Focus:

      PTE Core is designed to assess general English language skills for everyday situations, making it suitable for migration, work, and citizenship purposes, particularly for Canada 1. In contrast, PTE Academic focuses on academic English proficiency, primarily used for university admissions and student visas1 2

    • Test Content:

      PTE Core emphasizes real-life scenarios and practical language use. It includes tasks such as responding to everyday situations and writing emails.23 PTE Academic, on the other hand, includes more academically-oriented tasks like summarizing lectures and writing essays 4

    • Question Types:

      While both tests share many similar question types, there are some differences:

      • PTE Core replaces the "Retell Lecture" task from PTE Academic with a new "Respond to a Situation" task in the Speaking section2
      • PTE Core does not include the "Highlight Correct Summary" task found in PTE Academic.4
      • PTE Core features an "Write an Email" task instead of the "Essay" task in PTE Academic.3
    • Word Limits:

      Some tasks in PTE Core have different word limits compared to PTE Academic:

      • Summarize Written Text: 25-50 words in PTE Core vs. 5-75 words in PTE Academic.3
      • Write an Email: 50-120 words in PTE Core vs. 200-300 words for the Essay in PTE Academic4

    • PTE Core is slightly shorter, with a total duration of 2 hours, while PTE Academic typically takes about 3 hours to complete1 2

    • Scoring and Results:

      Both tests use AI and automated scoring systems, but PTE Core results are typically available within 2 days, which can be as quick as 24 hours in some cases. 2 PTE Academic results are usually available within 5 business days.

    • Acceptance:

      PTE Core is specifically accepted by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) for all immigration pathways, including the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP), Express Entry, and Canadian Citizenship. 2 PTE Academic is widely accepted for academic purposes and immigration to countries like Australia, New Zealand, and the UK23

    • Test Availability:

      PTE Core is a newer test, with bookings available from February 12, 2024, onwards. 2 PTE Academic has been available for longer and is more widely offered.

    Understanding these differences is crucial for test-takers to choose the appropriate test for their specific needs and to prepare effectively for the unique aspects of each exam.

    PTE Choice: Academic or Core

    When deciding between PTE Academic (regular PTE) and PTE Core, consider your specific goals and requirements:

    Choose PTE Academic if:

    1. You're applying for university admissions or student visas, particularly in countries like Australia, New Zealand, or the UK. PTE Academic is widely accepted for academic purposes in these countries. 1 2
    2. You need to demonstrate proficiency in academic English. PTE Academic focuses on language skills in an academic context, which is ideal for students planning to study abroad 2
    3. You're comfortable with longer test durations. PTE Academic typically takes about 3 hours to complete, allowing for more in-depth assessment. 3

    Choose PTE Core if:

    1. You're applying for Canadian immigration, work visas, or citizenship. PTE Core is specifically approved by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) for all immigration pathways, including the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP), Express Entry, and Canadian Citizenship. 3
    2. You need to demonstrate general English proficiency for everyday situations. PTE Core focuses on real-life scenarios and practical language use, making it more suitable for work and migration purposes. 1 2
    3. You prefer a shorter test duration. PTE Core is 2 hours long, which may be less stressful for some test-takers3
    4. You want faster results. PTE Core typically provides results within 2 days, sometimes as quickly as 24 hours.3
    5. You're more comfortable with tasks related to everyday situations. PTE Core includes tasks like responding to everyday scenarios and writing emails, which some test-takers may find more relatable.4 3

    1. Both tests are computer-based and use AI scoring, ensuring objectivity in evaluation. 1 3
    2. PTE Core is a newer test, so it may be less widely recognized outside of Canada compared to PTE Academic. 1
    3. If you're unsure about your future plans, PTE Academic might offer more flexibility as it's accepted for both academic and some immigration purposes in multiple countries. 2

    Ultimately, your choice should align with your immediate goals and the specific requirements of the institution or immigration program you're applying to. Always check the latest acceptance policies of your target country or institution before deciding.
